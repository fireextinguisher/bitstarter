Preorder

SkillExchange brings together qualified people and recruiters. Qualified person asks lowest considerable salary for his/her skill(s) while recruiter bids for required skills with highest salary offered (skills are just tags describing core expertise). Skill exchange is divided into sections according to preferred locations and level of expertise. Bidding process is anonymous until ask and bid match and both parties disclose their CV and Job description for a commission paid to the exchange. Suggested recruiting process enables continuous search for better opportunities in the job market and saves time by aligning salary expectations from the very beginning.

Candidates would anonymously ask a certain salary for a set of skills they have, and recruiters will anonymously place an order for the required skills at the maximum acceptable salary. After the match is identified and confirmed by both parties their profiles are disclosed.

Suggested recruiting process is intended to be quick, fun and straightforward - you trade your skills on open market and instantly understand if there is any interest in your offer from recruiters. For example, skill - "Ruby on Rails", preferred location - "Moscow", level of expertise - "Advanced (4 years or more)", Ask - "6,000 USD" net per month.

FAQ

Why do we believe people need what we're making?
We interview many people as a part of our full time job responsibilities. In the last year alone we had interviewed more than 80 candidates, and the hardest part of it is salary negotiations. It is not uncommon that we spend a lot of time on a candidate only to find out at the end that his expectations exceed what we can offer. So we have this idea to use stock exchange mechanisms to find a match between salary expectations of a candidate and employers' offer.
Who are your competitors, and who might become competitors?
We have not yet identified services that provide the opportunity to anonymously match candidates and job openings based on salary level expected. However, there are large online job openings databases like monster.com and salary surveying sites like salary.com, whatsalary.com and payscale.com. As soon as they merge their services, they become very strong competitor. The most dangerous competitor would be LinkedIn if they allow their users to add salary level in private section of profile and use that information to find suitable job openings.
What do you understand about your business that other companies in it just don't get?
Salary negotiation is most important and most stressful part of recruiting process with intermediary (recruiting consultant) between employer and candidate. Automated salary negotiation should make the recruiting process easier and cheaper.
How will you make money?
Participants of skill exchange pay commission for being able to see CV and Job description for matched ask and bid. Commission depends on salary expected/offered to ensure fair salary estimation (inexperienced person will have to pay higher commission with rather limited chances to be hired).
How will you get users?
We consider integration with LinkedIn to let qualified people and recruiters start "trading" almost instantly using their LinkedIn skills & expertise and job postings respectively.
